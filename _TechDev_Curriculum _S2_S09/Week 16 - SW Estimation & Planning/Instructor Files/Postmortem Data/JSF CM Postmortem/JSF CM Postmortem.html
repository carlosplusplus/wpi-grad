Lifecycle: The JSF CM software is following an Evolutionary Spiral Development lifecycle model. In this model, the software system is built in increments. Each increment is intended to be a slice of the complete system. For JSF, specific software capabilities are allocated to each spiral (known as a block) as agreed to with the customer -- each subsequent block builds upon the previous block, adding new functionality and/or enhancing existing functionality. The cycle is repeated until all software capabilities are fully functional. A feature of this lifecycle is that the full sequence of software development activities is performed in each spiral (ie., SwRA, DD, CUT and sw integration). The SW is delivered to Systems I&T in the spirals (blocks) as shown below: Block 0.5.1, 0.5.2, 0.5.3, 0.5.4 and 0.5.5 Block 1a, 1b, 1c and 1d Block 2a and 2b
Standards: No specific military standards were imposed. The BAE SYSTEMS OSSP was used on this project (ie., IPDP). This project is executing as CMM Level 4 for software, and CMM-I Level 3 for the general program.

Lessons Learned
Problem Complete JSF program replan mid-stream created a major challenge for the continuity of metrics and the generation of intermediate postmortems.
Phase Software Management
Cause The JSF underwent a program replan with the customeron 2-May-2005. New budgets and schedules were established. CAM's were able to replan the cost to complete the job by submiting estimates to the program office. New cost accounts and WBS's were established, along with new schedules and task descriptions. The program essentially reset itself and archived financial data before the replan.
Action It took several months to get the metrics to reflect what was really executing on the program. Even then, there was still a level of uncertainty in the metics data.
Impact Took a lot of effort to restructure the JSF sw metrics after the replan. DevProg was out of sync with the new tasks and hours budget, Codesize was out of sync, as new estimates were generated for much of the remaining work. The quad chart cost and hours info had to be changed. History and continuity were lost in some cases.
Recommendations Look into a way to facilitate a program replan in the Java Metric Tool. Or adopt a policy that should a program do a re-plan or re-start, we archive the metrics at that point and start a new set.


Problem Late requirements from Systems Engineering caused heavy schedule delays in software.
Phase SW Requirements Analysis
Cause Not enough Systems Engineering resources assigned to CM requirements for Block 1.
Action A Tiger Team was formed that included members from Systems Engineeing and SW Engineering to focus on developing and completing Block 1 requirements. Rules of engagement were developed by the SW and Systems CAM's. Tiger Team progress was monitored closely.
Impact Got off to a slow start. Spent a lot of time on 1 or 2 functional areas. After that, progress picked up and the requirements were completed.
Recommendations Monitor progress of hand-ins from Systems Engineering closeely. Raise potential schedule impacts from other organizations to the program office early. Put the hand-in on the critical path.
